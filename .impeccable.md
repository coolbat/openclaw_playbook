## Design Context

### Users
This template is for creators, indie builders, and small teams who need a bilingual tutorial or playbook site they can fork quickly. Their readers are usually trying to onboard into a product, workflow, or knowledge system with minimal friction, often moving between overview pages, step-by-step guides, templates, and troubleshooting content.

### Brand Personality
Clear, editorial, and pragmatic. The interface should feel structured and reliable, with enough warmth to avoid looking like a generic docs scaffold. It should communicate confidence, approachability, and a sense that the information architecture has already been thought through.

### Aesthetic Direction
Static-first tutorial brand with an editorial shell rather than a developer-docs shell. Warm light surfaces, restrained orange brand accents, compact chips, and high-contrast typography are preferred. Avoid shiny SaaS-dashboard tropes, dark neon gradients, glassy UI, and generic AI landing-page patterns. The site should feel closer to a well-designed learning publication than a component demo.

### Design Principles
1. Prioritize legibility over spectacle, especially for bilingual long-form reading.
2. Use typography and spacing to create hierarchy before adding more color or decoration.
3. Keep navigation and onboarding paths obvious; users should always know the next useful step.
4. Make template features visible in the UI, but keep them compact and practical.
5. Preserve the warm, editorial visual language while keeping the site easy to fork and rebrand.
