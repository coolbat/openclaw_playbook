## When to use this template

Use this when leadership needs a stable morning overview instead of raw feeds. The goal is not volume. The goal is to help a decision-maker see what changed overnight and what deserves attention.

## Minimal setup

Choose a small set of recurring signal sources and one delivery channel such as email or Feishu. Keep the structure consistent each morning so readers learn where to find the important parts quickly.

## What good output looks like

The output should answer three questions fast: what changed, why it matters, and whether anything needs a response today. If the brief feels like a newsletter, it is already too long.

## First extension

Once the baseline works, add a short watchlist section for signals that are not urgent today but may become important later in the week.
